Introduction

A Raga arises out of a structured combination of Swaras. There's a very specific sequence to each.

The notes S, P, S' are fixed for most ragas and for some, it is S, M, S'. It depends, really. There are exceptions.

The ascending ('Arohana') and descending sequences ('Arohana') are then split into smaller phrases to make a composition. You can practice these smaller phrases to get a brief of the Raga's Bhava (Emotion).

There are Ragas that consist of all the seven swaras. These are the complete ones with a lot of construct the Bhava with. We shall refer to a collection of these a Melakarta. This collection is a really cleverly defined scheme with a well structured combination and identification mechanism.

Other Ragas

As you might have guessed already, other ragas are mostly derivatives of these complete ragas from the Melakarta scheme. These derivatives ( Janya Ragas) are a bunch of 4 - 5 Swaras that can be intricately combined.

There are some terrific Ragas out there that have their own distinct personalities.

Some Ragas can have you fantasize about Indian snake charmers and some Ragas can get you lost in the mountains of Kashmir.

Raga Database

The Raga database is a collection of interesting and fun facts about some of the oh-so-many Ragas in the Carnatic encyclopedia.

As you expect, every entry here gives you the 'Arohana' and the 'Arohana' along with some sample phrases.

You can remember the correct keys and practice playing these phrases on the virtual piano. Do not hesitate to search for the name of the Raga on a search engine to hear artists perform compositions.
