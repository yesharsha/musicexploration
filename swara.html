Introduction to Swara

Swaras (pl): A collection of notes strung together to express a certain mood.

Common Sequence

Sa, Ri, Ga, Ma, Pa, Da, Ni and the octave is continued again by a Sa.

Types

There are 16 types of swaras.

WHAT? HOW?

Yes, a standard piano has only 12 notes in one octave. Some swaras are faux-notes. For example, the Ri notes are represented as R1, R2 and R3. R3 is a faux-note which was supposed to sound like a Ga.

How do I learn the Swaras? Do I need to memorize them?

Tinker around with the common sequence of Swaras (Sa, Ri, Ga, Ma, Pa, Da, Ni, Sa). Play them in two sequences: an ascending sequence and a descending sequence.

What's the deal with these sequences?

The set of ascending and descending sequences takes us to the concept of Ragas.

Eg. Sa, Ri, Ga, Ma, Pa, Da, Ni, Sa | Sa, Ni, Da, Pa, Ma, Ga, Ri, Sa ||
