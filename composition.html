Introduction

Composing music in Carnatic requires a good grasp of phrasing in Raga, Tala and good Vibes. You can choose to incorporate either of these elements to make music.

Search for these terms to get a quick understanding of what's been happening over the last few hundred years with Carnatic music.

Tyagaraja Dikshitar Shyama Sastri Purandaradasa

Kriti Varnam Pancharathna Kriti

Let's get started

Basically, all you need to make music using Carnatic theory on a computer is :

1. A continuous Drone (Shruti)

2. A Raga (a set of swaras) and a good grasp of it.

3. Rhythm, Atmosphere, and FX to add context.

Buckle up

And you also need a Digital Audio workstation

Like Ableton Live

Or something like FL Studio

Or if you're a musician on Apple computers, Logic Pro

and lots of samples and plugin libraries (VST).

Lay the stuff together.

Depending on whether you're making an ambient track filled with sounds of Foliage or maybe a templated genre like 'Jungle' or 'Dub', plan up your track.

Feel the mood and contemplate upon it, getting inspired with the Shruti drone running in the background.

Surround yourself with Higher and positive Vibrations and request your followers on twitter to pour out blessings on your feed instead of nasty war politics.

Lay out Atmosphere on the Shruti and top it up with some Rhythm, FX and proceed towards Melody.

Make short phrases from a Raga chosen according to the feel. Use the Raga Database to pick the right Raga. Lay these phrases on the Rhythm. Mix and match.

Add sound FX, polish the track, EQ it and compress based on intuition.

Perform all these steps in a Zen way and preserve the aesthetics at all times.

Finish the track, export into high quality formats and thank the Universe for the good vibes.
